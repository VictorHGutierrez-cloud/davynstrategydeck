Playbook summary (2 minutes)

Caribbean buyers prioritize risk, continuity, and trust — not slides full of generic features.

  • ICP & verticals: focus beats “every logo.”
  • Discovery: data residency, audits, multi‑island operations, international payments.
  • Objection handling: sovereignty, no IT headcount, price, Microsoft/Google coexistence.
  • 90-day rhythm: weekly call coaching + bi-weekly strategy on top deals.
  • Metrics in QBR: stage conversion, cycle length, demo→stage, ASP, loss reasons (picklist).

Read full playbook (EN · Markdown)

What Caribbean buyers worry about

  • Data sovereignty & control — who holds risk, audits, residency.
  • Operational continuity — hurricanes, dependencies, local support.
  • Talent scarcity — they need a crisp rollout plan.
  • Financial friction — FX, digital services tax, procurement — when relevant.

7-day checklist (with your PAE)

Pipeline & predictability
  • Top 10 active deals with stage + dated next step + owner.
  • Written ICP.
  • Stage conversion (even spreadsheet math).
  • Loss reasons standardized.
Sales capability
  • Business discovery vs feature dumps?
  • Stakeholder maps per deal?
  • Mutual plans with dated exec commitments?
Enablement starter kit
  • Three proof points ready to share.
  • Five objections scripted (see below).

Common objections — short answers

Buyer says… Steer toward…
“We can’t host data abroad.” Governance workshop (controls, RACI, records) — involve Legal & IT early.
“We lack IT bandwidth.” Assisted rollout + weekly checkpoints + quantify cost of spreadsheets.
“Too expensive.” Total cost of duplicate tools + rework + compliance drag — anchor on 3 customer numbers.
“We already pay for Microsoft/Google.” Separate workforce operations (policies, HR workflows, approvals) from general productivity suites.
