# Partner playbook — Improving conversion with Davyn (Caribbean focus)

**Audience:** Partner Account Executive (or equivalent) working with **Davyn**, and Davyn’s revenue leaders.  
**Goal:** Improve conversion with **predictability** (repeatable process), not “more random collateral.”  
**Data note:** CRM export snapshot dated **2026-05-27** (baseline — always validate live in CRM).

---

## 1) Executive summary

In the Caribbean, B2B SaaS buying is often driven by **risk, operational continuity, and regional trust** — not feature lists.

Themes that show up repeatedly:

- **Data sovereignty / control** and concerns about extraterritorial regimes  
- **Operational continuity** (disasters, dependence on vendors, local support)  
- **Limited technical capacity** → buyers need clear implementation and operating paths  
- **Financial friction** (international subscriptions, digital services tax, FX controls) → “how we pay / how we invoice” can stall deals  

**What this means:** your job isn’t a product manual — it’s helping Davyn sound like a **regional consulting partner**: strong discovery, urgency tied to operational risk/cost, weekly cadence.

## 3) ICP & priority verticals

**Rule:** fewer “spray-and-pray” accounts; more accounts where the **regional storyline matches pain**.

### Profiles that often fit

- Hospitality / resorts / seasonal ops  
- Finance / regulated services (data + audit sensitivity)  
- Telecom & local integrators  
- Private healthcare & education  
- Multi‑island groups  

### Vertical → pain → outcome → discovery prompts (examples)

**Hospitality**
- Pain: high turnover, slow onboarding  
- Outcome: faster HR ops, fewer errors  
- Ask: how do you onboard today? time-to-productivity? who approves exceptions?

**Finance / audit‑heavy**
- Pain: traceability & permissions  
- Outcome: smoother audits  
- Ask: how do you prove approvals? where are records? non‑negotiable data requirements?

**Multi‑island groups**
- Pain: tool sprawl  
- Outcome: one system of record for people/process  
- Ask: how many HR tools? what does HQ need monthly from each island?

## 4) Playbooks

### 4.1 “Regional-first” discovery flow

1. Ops context: how is the workforce managed today; where is fragmentation?  
2. Risk & data: audit, residency, subprocessors, vendors.  
3. Capacity: who operates the system (internal IT vs partner vs hybrid)?  
4. Finance/compliance: invoicing, digital services tax, international payments (when relevant).  
5. Stakeholders & decision process: real timeline, committee, legal/procurement.  
6. Economic value: rework, errors, manager time, turnover (estimates are ok).  

**Output each call:** stakeholder map + risks + dated next step.

### 4.2 Objection battlecards (commercial guidance — not legal advice)

| Objection | Short response |
|-----------|----------------|
| “We don’t want data outside the region” | Shift from “cloud yes/no” to **governance**: controls, segregation, records, backups, roles. Run a **workshop** with Legal/IT early. |
| “We don’t have technical people” | Offer **assisted rollout** + weekly cadence + change checklist; quantify **cost of doing nothing**. |
| “It’s expensive vs X” | Compare **total cost**: parallel tools, rework, audit pain, payroll mistakes, internal churn. Ask for 3 numbers. |
| “We already have Microsoft/Google” | Separate **productivity** from **HR/process system**: policies, workflows, executive reporting. |
| “Maybe next budget cycle” | Negotiate a **pilot** with exit criteria + decision date (mutual plan). |
| “Hurricanes / continuity” | Operational **business continuity**: ownership, support, export/offline responsibilities. |

## 5) Enablement ritual (90 days)

**Weekly (60–90 min):** review 3 calls + 15‑min roleplay on 1 hard objection + ship 1 mini‑asset.  
**Bi‑weekly:** deal strategy on top 5 (stakeholders, risk, exec next step, mutual plan).  
**Monthly:** refresh ICP, messaging, objections.
